Large Language Models, Small Labor Market Effects

Groundbreaking field study of 25,000 Danish workers across 11 AI-exposed occupations, tracked through 18 months of administrative earnings data, reveals the gap between AI adoption and economic impact. Despite 83% adoption with employer encouragement, 38% of firms deploying in-house models, and 30% of employees receiving training, AI chatbots have had precisely estimated zero effects on earnings, hours, and wages—with confidence intervals ruling out impacts greater than 1%. Users report modest productivity gains (2.8% average time savings), but only 3–7% passes through to wages due to weak bargaining power, task-output mismatches, and monopsony frictions. No firm-level effects on employment or wage bills detected. The GenAI labor market transformation is occurring on a years-long timescale, not quarters.
