Conversational AI increases political knowledge as effectively as self-directed internet search

Lennart Luettgau, Hannah Kirk, Kobi Hackenburg, Christopher Summerfield et al. • arXiv:2509.05219 (Sep 2025)

View paper ->

The authors combine a nationally representative UK survey with randomized trials that pit GPT-4o, Claude 3.5, and Mistral chatbots against traditional search. They find that voters already lean on AI for election research, that guardrailed models lift belief in true facts and suppress misinformation just as much as search, and that even persuasive or sycophantic prompts fail to shift attitudes.

🧭

Executive quick take

Chatbots are already a mainstream election research tool: roughly one in eight UK voters turned to them in 2024. In controlled trials they match web search at boosting true beliefs, do not amplify misinformation, and stay neutral even when pushed toward persuasion, suggesting that modern guardrails are working under everyday use.

  • Adoption reality: 32% of chatbot users applied them to political decisions during the UK general election campaign.
  • Epistemic parity: Conversational AI lifted agreement with true facts by ~0.7 Likert points and nudged misinformation down just like search.
  • Prompt resilience: Persuasive and sycophantic system prompts delivered statistically negligible attitude shifts.

Usage snapshot

Surveyed UK voters leaned on AI for the same tasks they once reserved for search.

  • 9% of adults used chatbots for political info in the four weeks around polling day.
  • Chatbot users ranked politics alongside work and education as top scenarios.
  • Perceived bias split evenly left vs right, hinting at balanced guardrails.

Epistemic impact

The trials emphasise belief change, not just anecdotal answer quality.

  • True statements gained ~0.7 Likert points post-research across both channels.
  • Misinformation belief dropped similarly, confirming symmetrical guardrails.
  • No measurable drift in trust, private beliefs, or extremism indices.

Terminology

  • Conversational AI condition: Guided sessions with GPT-4o, Claude 3.5, or Mistral using up-to-date guardrails.
  • Internet search control: Participants free-roamed via search engines to research the same prompts.
  • Persuasive/sycophantic prompts: System messages that force agreement with a random or user-held stance.
  • Epistemic health measures: Likert-scale agreement with curated true and false statements, plus trust, belief, and extremism composites.

🧪 Experiments & Evidence

  • Post-election survey: Weighted UK sample (N=2,499) detailing chatbot adoption, perceived accuracy, and bias.
  • Four-issue RCTs: 2,858 participants researched climate, immigration, criminal justice, and COVID policy via AI or search.
  • Prompt interventions: Persuasive and sycophantic system prompts showed no significant divergence from neutral GPT-4o.
  • Latency readout: Conversational AI shortened task completion time by ~2 minutes versus search.

🔭 Forward-looking roadmap

  • Replicate in other electorates and longer campaign windows to watch for drift.
  • Instrument production chatbots to detect when prompts exceed tested guardrail regimes.
  • Blend conversational and retrieval experiences to preserve guardrails while exposing primary sources.
  • Develop monitoring for persuasion attempts that combine multiple tool calls or external links.
