Genius on Demand: The Value of Transformative Artificial Intelligence

Ajay Agrawal, Joshua S. Gans, Avi Goldfarb • NBER Transformative AI Conference (Sep 2025)

View paper ↗

Picture a service firm with two kinds of problem solvers: most people follow established playbooks, and a tiny "genius" crew invents fresh answers when the work does not fit any template. The paper explores what happens when AI tools can act like extra geniuses on call.

In this setup, a human genius is the person who can always improvise a solution but spends more effort the stranger the request. Routine teammates stick to the scripts they already know, so their answers get shaky once an issue drifts too far from familiar ground.

Executive quick take

Treat transformative AI as a surge of extra experts. Use it to cover the repetitive middle while your scarce humans double down on frontier questions. If AI keeps improving, routine roles will shrink unless you slow deployment or find new tasks for those teams.

  • Smart routing beats raw brainpower: The win comes from steering each request to the right helper, not from hiring the "smartest" person overall.
  • Humans keep the weird stuff: They shine on messy, one-off situations where context and judgement matter.
  • AI eats the middle tier: Once AI handles mid-scope questions with similar cost and accuracy, routine teams have little left to do.

Business relevance

  • Professional services COOs: Redesign staffing ladders so senior experts see only the handful of cases that truly need creative thinking.
  • Enterprise knowledge ops: Tag tickets by "distance from the playbook" and let AI auto-resolve anything near the centre.
  • Labour planning leads: Build reskilling programs and guardrails now if you want to preserve routine analyst roles.

Derivative example (claims triage reboot)

An insurance claims team tags each incoming case by how familiar it looks. AI helpers settle the straightforward claims automatically, while veteran adjusters handle the edge cases with a short AI brief in hand.

Key idea

Save human genius time for questions sitting right at the edge of what the organisation already knows.

How the model works

Routine workers do great close to the playbook. Geniuses can solve anything but take longer the further they travel. A manager decides who gets each request.

What changes with AI

AI acts like extra geniuses. First it backfills mid-scope work, then it pushes humans to the outer frontier, and eventually it can leave routine staff idle.

How managers triage today

List the incoming issues from "closest to the playbook" to "completely new." Give your limited geniuses the ones that would stump the routine crew.

  • Routine answers fade steadily as problems drift away from what is already documented.
  • Geniuses stay helpful further out, but you cannot afford to use them everywhere.
  • The sweet spot is to carve out a band of tough questions that only the geniuses touch.

What floods in with AI

Add AI copilots and the mix flips. They first handle the near-playbook work the geniuses used to babysit, then they start competing with routine staff, and finally only the weirdest edge cases stay human-led.

  • Short run: Humans retreat outward while AI takes over their easier tickets.
  • Long run: AI covers nearly everything that once belonged to routine teams.
  • Routine roles survive only if you slow adoption or keep humans on core workflows for trust or compliance reasons.

Evidence base

  • Mathematical triage rule that ranks requests by how much extra value a genius adds versus a routine worker.
  • Scenario analysis showing how different levels of AI efficiency shift the human/AI split.
  • Illustrations that compare the pre-AI, short-run, and long-run staffing patterns.
  • Discussion about how the theory maps to everyday settings like support desks and consulting teams.

Forward-looking roadmap

  • Measure what share of work is truly bespoke versus playbook-driven.
  • Pilot co-pilot flows where AI drafts answers and humans review only the outliers.
  • Set explicit guardrails for regulated domains before AI displaces too much human oversight.
  • Track staffing, pay, and training needs for frontier experts as AI picks up more of the middle.
