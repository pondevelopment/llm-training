We Won't Be Missed: Work and Growth in the Era of AGI

Pascual Restrepo • NBER Transformative AI Conference (Jul 2025)

View paper ↗

Restrepo models an economy where artificial general intelligence (AGI) can execute every economically valuable task using compute. The core result: once compute can cover all growth-bottleneck work, output is paced purely by how fast compute expands, not by the number of people available.

The chapter separates work into bottleneck tasks that cap growth and accessory tasks that can be dropped without slowing progress. As AGI absorbs the bottlenecks, wages track the compute cost of reproducing human effort and labor's share of GDP trends toward zero, even though some people keep working in accessory roles.

Plain-language explainer

Imagine a factory where robots can do every critical step so long as you feed them enough electricity. Humans stick around only when it's cheaper to pay someone than to spin up more compute, or when the task is optional anyway. Restrepo's model shows how that world rewrites wage setting, profit shares, and growth forecasts.

Executive quick take

Treat compute as the binding growth input in an AGI world. Capital plans must budget for exponential compute growth, while people strategy shifts to cushioning the income shock from vanishing labor shares.

  • Bottleneck triage: Map which workflows actually limit growth; expect AGI to eat these first.
  • Wages shadow compute: Pay converges to the cost of renting the compute needed to replicate a worker.
  • Income reallocation: Owners of compute capture the surplus; policy must decide how it is recycled.
  • Scientific flywheel: Once AGI runs research bottlenecks, productivity growth tracks compute expansion without a runaway singularity.

Business relevance

Compute allocation discipline

AGI transforms capacity planning into a compute budgeting problem. Every human in a bottleneck role must justify their seat versus the compute credits needed to automate them.

  • COOs: Decide when to reserve human talent for scarce accessory tasks versus redirecting spend into compute scale-out.
  • CFOs: Model wage floors using cloud or on-prem compute prices; renegotiate contracts as hardware costs shift.
  • People teams: Prepare compensation policies tied to compute benchmarks and build safety nets for roles losing value.

Business playbook examples

  • Autonomous cloud ops: Benchmark site reliability engineers against the GPU hours required for AGI runbooks before converting teams.
  • Customer care: Keep human concierge pods for high-value clients while routing the long tail through AGI agents tuned to service SLAs.
  • Design labs: Let AGI own concept exploration, redeploy humans to taste-making or regulatory liaison work that compute cannot justify yet.
Key insight

Once AGI handles every growth-critical task, GDP grows linearly with compute and labor's income share collapses toward zero.

Method

Task-allocation model splitting bottleneck and accessory work, with compute and labor substituting subject to resource constraints, extended to semi-endogenous growth for R&D.

Implications

Forecast wages using compute prices, design dividends or ownership plans for compute capital, and expect policy fights over who controls AGI infrastructure.

Measurement priorities

Track how close you are to the AGI steady state.

  • Bottleneck inventory: Maintain a live map of tasks that still constrain throughput and their compute replacement cost.
  • Compute pricebook: Publish internal reference prices for key AGI workloads (training, inference, scientific discovery).
  • Labor vs compute spend: Trend the ratio over time; convergence signals the tipping point where wages mirror compute costs.

Policy signals

How regulators and boards should respond.

  • Compute access: Debate public options or dividend schemes once compute rents dominate income.
  • Transition supports: Pair wage insurance with compute vouchers so displaced workers can spin up AGI services.
  • Risk pacing: Consider gradual deployment mandates where wholesale automation would trigger macro shocks.
  • Meaning and agency: Pair income redistribution with new civic or creative roles so people retain purpose once bottleneck labour disappears.

Evidence base

  • Proof that any bottleneck task eventually shifts to AGI as compute scales, while accessory work can remain human without throttling growth.
  • Result that wages converge to the compute opportunity cost and labor's GDP share vanishes in the steady state.
  • Extension showing AGI-run science pins long-run productivity growth to compute expansion instead of population dynamics.

Forward-looking roadmap

  • Set compute dividend pilots that recycle surplus from AGI workloads to employees and communities.
  • Prototype mixed compute-human pods for accessory work so you know which jobs persist post-automation.
  • Instrument your cost accounting so every new hire ships with a compute shadow price and displacement plan.
  • Coordinate with policymakers on transparency for compute capacity, usage audits, and emergency throttles.
