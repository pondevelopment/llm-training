Derivative example (Incentive alignment review)

Run a quarterly workshop where each VP lists their top three desired behaviors and the actual rewards attached (bonuses, dashboards, shout-outs). Map mismatches on a heatmap, assign owners to redesign the reward, and track leading indicators (quality audits, renewal uplift, cycle times) after the change.

Why misaligned incentives persist

  • Organizations default to measuring what is easy (counts, deadlines) instead of what is meaningful.
  • Legacy reward systems survive leadership changes because no one owns sunsetting them.
  • Cognitive dissonance: teams convince themselves that symbolic rewards will somehow produce stretch goals.

How to run the audit

  • List the outcomes you truly need (B) and gather evidence of what actually gets rewarded (A).
  • Score each reward on immediacy, visibility, and payout magnitude to see which behaviors it amplifies.
  • Prototype new incentives with small cohorts; verify they change behavior before scaling firm-wide.
