The realistic spread of SSR-generated ratings enables valid statistical analysis and reliable prediction of aggregate consumer behavior.

üîë Key findings

  • Distribution realism: SSR achieves KS similarity > 0.85 to human responses across all 57 test surveys, while direct numerical ratings produce unrealistic distributions due to extreme value biases.
  • High reliability: SSR reaches approximately 90% of human test-retest reliability, demonstrating consistent synthetic respondent behavior across repeated measurements.
  • Scale efficiency: Generate 10,000 synthetic responses in hours compared to weeks for equivalent human panels; marginal cost approaches zero after initial setup.
  • Qualitative richness: Every SSR rating includes interpretable textual explanation, enabling thematic analysis and feature importance mining unavailable in traditional surveys.
  • Broad applicability: Tested on personal care products but methodology generalizes to any domain using Likert scales‚Äîelectronics, food, services, concepts, etc.
