Legal & Business Implications

Copyright fair-use impact: When readers prefer AI-generated derivatives at 0.3% production cost, the fourth fair-use factor ("effect upon potential market") shifts from theoretical to quantified. Courts now have empirical evidence of market substitution.

Detection impossibility: With fine-tuned outputs evading AI detectors (3% vs 97% for in-context), enforcement strategies must shift from technical detection to licensing frameworks, attribution requirements, and economic compensation models.

Economic displacement: The 99.7% cost reduction combined with reader preference creates immediate competitive pressure on professional writers. Content organizations face strategic choices: adopt AI-augmented workflows, emphasize human creativity AI cannot replicate, or invest in hybrid editorial processes.

Study methodology

Participants: 159 expert and lay readers conducting blind pairwise evaluations.

Materials: 450-word excerpts from 50 award-winning authors' writing styles, generated by three frontier models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) and MFA-trained expert human writers.

Conditions: In-context prompting provides basic style examples; fine-tuning uses each author's complete published works for training.

Measures: Stylistic fidelity ("captures author's voice"), writing quality ("would recommend"), AI detection accuracy using best available detectors.

Analysis: Mediation analysis isolates stylistic quirks (cliche density, formulaic markers) explaining preference shifts between conditions.

Note: Values shown are from the preregistered study (Chakrabarty et al., 2025). Odds ratios compare AI outputs to expert human writers; values >1 favor AI, <1 favor humans. Statistical significance levels indicate confidence in observed effects.
