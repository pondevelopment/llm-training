Key insights

Why the gap between productivity and pay?

The weak pass-through (3‚Äì7%) from time savings to earnings reflects three labor market realities:

  • Task vs. output mismatch: Finishing existing tasks faster doesn't increase valuable output unless workflows reorganize to assign more high-impact work‚Äîwhich rarely happens without employer coordination.
  • Weak bargaining power: Bottom-up tool adoption (workers choosing to use ChatGPT) doesn't create credible negotiating leverage the way employer-sponsored training or certifications do. Workers can't easily claim raises for using a free public tool.
  • Monopsony frictions: Search costs, non-compete agreements, and geographic constraints mean workers can't easily switch employers to capture their productivity gains. Employers retain most of the surplus.

When pass-through is higher (7% vs. 3%): Encouraged workplaces show better pass-through because (1) employers acknowledge tool benefits and may reward high performers, (2) coordinated adoption enables workflow redesign that increases marginal productivity, and (3) training programs create more credentialable skills.
