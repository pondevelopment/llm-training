What drives horizon growth?

Analysis of GPT-4 vs. o1 failures reveals three key drivers: reliability (o1 repeats failed actions 10× less often), self-awareness (knowing when to abandon intractable tasks), and compound improvements in tool use, logical reasoning, and error correction.

Horizon growth isn't just about model scale—it's about better scaffolding, smarter recovery strategies, and the ability to recognize task boundaries. These qualitative improvements compound into exponential capability gains.

Key takeaway

If the 7-month doubling trend persists, month-long autonomous tasks (167 work hours) arrive between late 2028 and early 2031—approximately 8 more doublings from today's frontier. Even if absolute horizons are off by 10×, the exponential structure suggests transformative automation within a decade. External validity (messiness, domain shift) remains the critical uncertainty.
