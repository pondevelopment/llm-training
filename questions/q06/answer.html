🌙️ What is temperature?

Temperature is the creativity dial for language models. It rescales logits before softmax: low values sharpen the distribution so the model acts cautious, while high values flatten it and let adventurous tokens surface. It is the quickest lever for balancing coherence and novelty.

Low temperature (0.1–0.5)

Conservative, laser-focused token choices.

✅ Coherent: Sticks to the prompt.
✅ Reliable: Minimal surprises.
❌ Repetitive: Can loop phrases.
❌ Flat tone: Creative flair disappears.
Best for: factual answers, summaries
Medium temperature (0.7–1.2)

Balanced mix of coherence and variation.

✅ Natural: Reads like human prose.
✅ Engaging: Adds light surprises.
✅ Versatile: Works for most tasks.
âš ️ Prompt sensitive: Needs guardrails.
Best for: chat, narratives, ideation
High temperature (1.5–2.0)

Experimental, high-entropy sampling.

✅ Creative: Unexpected combinations.
✅ Diverse: Wide vocabulary usage.
❌ Noisy: Logic quickly degrades.
❌ Risky: Harder to moderate.
Best for: poetry, brainstorming, prompts

📌 Why temperature matters

  • Mathematical control: Directly shapes the softmax output and entropy.
  • User experience: Lets product teams tune tone, creativity, and repeatability.
  • Safety and guardrails: Lower temperatures reduce the chance of off-policy responses.
  • Evaluation insights: Temperature sweeps expose model strengths and failure modes.

ℹ️ Practical notes

  • Pair temperature with top-k/p sampling or frequency penalties to fine-tune diversity.
  • Expose preset values (e.g., 0.2, 0.8, 1.5) so operators can A/B output styles quickly.
  • Log the temperature alongside prompts to debug inconsistent behaviour.
